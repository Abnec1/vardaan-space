I finally got a clean forecast and took the 135mm lens out for a proper first-light run. The goal was simple: test framing workflow, evaluate star shape at the corners, and get repeatable focus fast.

### What worked

- A short Bahtinov mask routine got me to critical focus in under three minutes.
- Framing with a simple two-star alignment was enough for this focal length.
- Stacking tolerance looked great across 90-second subs.

### What I need to improve

- Dew control needs an upgrade for long sessions.
- My capture plan should include a strict meridian-flip checkpoint.
- I still need a cleaner calibration frame routine.

Even with those gaps, this was the first session where everything felt repeatable. That's a good sign.
