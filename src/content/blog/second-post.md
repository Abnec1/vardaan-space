With only a few moonless hours available, I tested a strict target schedule instead of chasing whatever looked good in the moment.

1. Start with a bright target while transparency stabilizes.
2. Move to the main nebula target at peak altitude.
3. Close with a quick wide-field scene before dawn glow.

The sequence worked better than expected. I came back with usable signal on all three targets, even though none of them were "deep projects."

The biggest lesson: clear priorities beat spontaneity when sky time is limited.

Next session I want to automate this schedule with target altitudes precomputed in my planning notes.
