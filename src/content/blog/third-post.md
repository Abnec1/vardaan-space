I tend to overprocess when I'm excited about faint detail. This week I forced myself to use a compact sequence that protects star color first.

### Sequence I used

1. Gradient correction and neutralization.
2. Mild nonlinear stretch in two small passes.
3. Saturation boost using star mask protection.
4. Local contrast only on nebula regions.

The final image kept more of the original star palette, and the scene still gained enough structure to read clearly on a dark background.

I'm saving this sequence as my default baseline for future projects.
